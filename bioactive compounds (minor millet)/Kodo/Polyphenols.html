Polyphenols present in Kodo millet

S.No. COMPOUND NAME IUPAC NAME MOLECULAR FORMULA STRUCTURE SYNONYMS SOURCES HEALTH BENEFITS REFERENCE
1 Catechin (2R,3S)-2-(3,4-dihydroxyphenyl)-3,4-dihydro-2H-chromene-3,5,7-triol C15H14O6 Catechuic acid, Catechinic acid, Cyanidanol, D-Catechin,Cianidol, (+)-Catechol, (+)-Cyanidanol, Biocatechin. High concentrations of catechin can be found in red wine, broad beans, black grapes, apricots and strawberries; epicatechin concentrations are high in apples, blackberries, broad beans, cherries, black grapes, pears, raspberries, and chocolate. It has been proved that catechins could inhibit carcinogenesis, tumor growth, cancer cell invasion, and tumor angiogenesis, by suppressing the induction of proangiogenic factors, such as vascular endothelial growth factor (VEGF). [1]
2 Naringin (2S)-7-[(2S,3R,4S,5S,6R)-4,5-dihydroxy-6-(hydroxymethyl)-3-[(2S,3R,4R,5R,6S)-3,4,5-trihydroxy-6-methyloxan-2-yl]oxyoxan-2-yl]oxy-5-hydroxy-2-(4-hydroxyphenyl)-2,3-dihydrochromen-4-one C27H32O14 Naringoside, aurantiin, Naringenin 7-O-neohesperidoside, 4'5-diOH-Flavone-7-rhgluc Naringenin is a flavonoid belonging to flavanones subclass. It is widely distributed in several Citrus fruits, bergamot, tomatoes and other fruits, being also found in its glycosides form (mainly naringin). Naringenin is endowed with broad biological effects on human health , which includes a decrease in lipid peroxidation biomarkers and protein carbonylation, promotes carbohydrate metabolism, increases antioxidant defenses, scavenges reactive oxygen species, modulates immune system activity, and also exerts anti-atherogenic and anti-inflammatory effects. [2]
3 Taxifolin (2R,3R)-2-(3,4-dihydroxyphenyl)-3,5,7-trihydroxy-2,3-dihydrochromen-4-one C15H12O7 Taxifoliol, (2R,3R)-Dihydroquercetin 24198-97-8, (+)-Dihydroquercetin . It is found in onion, milk thistle, French maritime pine bark and Douglas fir bark. It is also used in various commercial preparations like Legalon™, Pycnogenol®, and Venoruton®. Taxifolin has shown to have anti-proliferative effects on many types of cancer cells by inhibiting cancer cell lipogenesis. By inhibiting the fatty acid synthase in cancer cells, taxifolin is able to prevent the growth and spread of cancer cells. [3]
4 Ferulic acid (E)-3-(4-hydroxy-3-methoxyphenyl)prop-2-enoic acid C10H10O4 4-Hydroxy-3-methoxycinnamic acid, trans-4-Hydroxy-3-methoxycinnamic acid, 3-(4-Hydroxy-3-methoxyphenyl)acrylic acid, (E)-Ferulic acid, Coniferic acid . It is commonly found in commelinid plants (rice, wheat, oats, and pineapple), grasses, grains, vegetables, flowers, fruits, leaves, beans, seeds of coffee, artichoke, peanut and nuts. It exhibits a wide range of therapeutic effects against various diseases like cancer, diabetes, cardiovascular and neurodegenerative. [4]
5 p-Coumaric acid (E)-3-(4-hydroxyphenyl)prop-2-enoic acid C9H8O3 p-Hydroxycinnamic acid, trans-4-Hydroxycinnamic acid, 4-Coumaric acid, 3-(4-hydroxyphenyl)acrylic acid, Para-Coumaric acid, Hydroxycinnamic acid. p-Coumaric acid can be found in a wide variety of edible plants and fungi such as peanuts, navy beans, tomatoes, carrots, basil and garlic. It is found in wine and vinegar. It is also found in barley grain. p-Coumaric acid can attenuate skin hyperpigmentation through multiple mechanisms. UV and other pathophysiological conditions stimulate the production of reactive oxygen species (ROS) and multiple signaling pathways leading to enhanced gene expression of tyrosinase and increased melanin synthesis. [5]
6 Sinapic acid (E)-3-(4-hydroxy-3,5-dimethoxyphenyl)prop-2-enoic acid C11H12O5 3,5-Dimethoxy-4-hydroxycinnamic acid, trans-sinapic acid, SINAPINATE, (E)-Sinapic acid, Sinapate, Synapoic acid. Sinapic acid is found in various edible plants such as spices, citrus and berry fruits, vegetables [10–12], cereals, and oilseed crops. Sinapic Acid’s health benefits extend beyond strengthening bone: Antimicrobial Activity ,Neuroprotective Properties ,Reducing Hyperglycemia . [6]
7 Pterin-6-Carboxylic acid 2-amino-4-oxo-3H-pteridine-6-carboxylic acid C7H5N5O3 2-amino-4-oxo-1H-pteridine-6-carboxylic acid, 2-amino-4-oxo-3,4-dihydropteridine-6-carboxylic acid, 2-Amino-1,4-dihydro-4-oxopteridine-6-carboxylic acid, 2-Amino-4-hydroxypteridine-6-carboxylicacid. Pterin-6-carboxylic acid is a natural product found in Capsicum annuum var. annuum. It penetrates into human skin after topical application, and its daily application improves skin elasticity, firmness, and the appearance of aged. [7]
