Polyphenols present in Barnyard millet

S.No. COMPOUND NAME IUPAC NAME MOLECULAR FORMULA STRUCTURE SYNONYMS SOURCES HEALTH BENEFITS REFERENCE
1 Gallic acid 3,4,5-trihydroxybenzoic acid C7H6O5 Benzoic acid, 3,4,5-trihydroxy-Gallic acid, Pyrogallol-5-carboxylic acid, Kyselina gallova, 3,4,5-Trihydroxybenzoate, 05 CHEBI:30778, AI3-16412, Kyselina 3,4,5-trihydroxybenzoova etc. Blueberry, blackberry, strawberry, plums, grapes, mango, cashew nut, hazelnut, walnut, tea, wine. Therapeutic activities in gastrointestinal, neuropsychological, metabolic, and cardiovascular disorders. [1,2]
2 Vanillic acid 4-hydroxy-3-methoxybenzoic acid C8H8O4 Acide vanllique Benzoic aci, 4-hydroxy-3-methoxy- p-Vanillic acid, 4-hydroxy- Protocatechuic acid, 3-methyl ester 4-hydroxy-3-methoxy-Benzoic acid etc. It is found in several fruits, olives, and cereal grains (e.g., whole wheat), as well as in wine, beer, and cider. Vanillic acid exerts diverse bioactivity against cancer, diabetes, obesity, neurodegenerative, cardiovascular, and hepatic diseases by inhibition of the associated molecular pathways. [3,4]
3 Ferulic acid (E)-3-(4-hydroxy-3-methoxyphenyl)prop-2-enoic acid C10H10O4 4-Hydroxy-3-methoxycinnamic acid, trans-4-Hydroxy-3-methoxycinnamic acid, 3-(4-Hydroxy-3-methoxyphenyl)acrylic acid, (E)-Ferulic acid, Coniferic acid . It is commonly found in commelinid plants (rice, wheat, oats, and pineapple), grasses, grains, vegetables, flowers, fruits, leaves, beans, seeds of coffee, artichoke, peanut and nuts. It exhibits a wide range of therapeutic effects against various diseases like cancer, diabetes, cardiovascular and neurodegenerative. [5]
4 p-Coumaric acid (E)-3-(4-hydroxyphenyl)prop-2-enoic acid C9H8O3 p-Hydroxycinnamic acid, trans-4-Hydroxycinnamic acid, 4-Coumaric acid, 3-(4-hydroxyphenyl)acrylic acid, Para-Coumaric acid, Hydroxycinnamic acid. p-Coumaric acid can be found in a wide variety of edible plants and fungi such as peanuts, navy beans, tomatoes, carrots, basil and garlic. It is found in wine and vinegar. It is also found in barley grain. p-Coumaric acid can attenuate skin hyperpigmentation through multiple mechanisms. UV and other pathophysiological conditions stimulate the production of reactive oxygen species (ROS) and multiple signaling pathways leading to enhanced gene expression of tyrosinase and increased melanin synthesis. [6]
5 Caffeic acid (E)-3-(3,4-dihydroxyphenyl)prop-2-enoic acid C9H8O4 (E)-3,4-dihydroxycinnamic acid, 2-Propenoic acid, 3-(3,4-dihydroxyphenyl)- trans-Caffeate, 4-(2-Carboxyethenyl)-1,2-dihydroxybenzene, 3,4-Dihydroxy-trans-cinnamate, 3-(3,4-Dihydroxyphenyl)propenoic acid, 4-(2'-Carboxyvinyl)-1,2-dihydroxybenzene . Caffeic acid (CA) is a polyphenol produced through the secondary metabolism of vegetables, (1–4) including olives, coffee beans, fruits, potatoes, carrots and propolis, and constitutes the main hydroxycinnamic acid found in the diet of humans (1, 3–5). As a natural product, it possesses several biological and physiological activities. These activities include antioxidant, anti-mutagenic, anti-tumor and anti-obesity effects. [7]
6 Chlorogenic acid (1S,3R,4R,5R)-3-[(E)-3-(3,4-dihydroxyphenyl)prop-2-enoyl]oxy-1,4,5-trihydroxycyclohexane-1-carboxylic acid C16H18O9 3-O-Caffeoylquinic acid, Chlorogenate, Heriguard, 3-(3,4-Dihydroxycinnamoyl)quinic acid, 3-Caffeoylquinic acid, Hlorogenic acid, CP chlorogenic acid, Caffeoyl quinic acid . Chlorogenic acid can be found in foods and herbs such as apples , artichoke , betel , burdock , carrots , coffee beans , eggplants , eucommia , grapes , honeysuckle , kiwi fruit , pears , plums , potatoes , tea. Chlorogenic acid has shown beneficial effects against diabetes in animals regarding blood glucose levels, lipid metabolism, cataracts, and wound- healing. [8]
