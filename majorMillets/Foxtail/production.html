Production

Foxtail millet predominates all millets in terms of productivity, yielding about 2166 kg/ha followed by Finger millet (1623 kg/ha), Proso millet (1535 kg/ha), Sorghum (1426 kg/ha), Barnyard millet (1034 kg/ha), Pearl millet (850 kg/ha), Little millet (469 kg/ha) and Kodo millet (419 kg/ha) . According to FAO, the world production of millets is 89.17 million metric tonnes from an area of 74.00 million hectare during 2020. Sorghum and Pearl millet are the major millet crops grown, constituting above 90% of the world millets production followed by Finger millet, Foxtail millet, Proso millet, Barnyard, Little millet and Kodo millet

Value added products :-

    1.Puffs :-

    It is ready to eat snack which is prepared using puff gun machine . Machine is loaded with dehulled foxtail grain onto a rotating barrel and the mixture is roasted for and fired . The Yield of Puffs – 92% and By-product yield – 82% .The puffs made from foxtail are white in colour and are crispy in nature, similar to the puffed rice. The shelf life is for 2 months when packed in air tight at ambient temperatures . Puffs are rich in protein and fibre. It can be in different Varieties masala and fried and it can serve as evening snack .

2.Chapati

Wheat and foxtail millet are mixed for making chapatti (roti). Gluten content is reduced in this mixture while making of flattering chapatti is not affected. Fortification of foxtail millet in chapattis is used for improving the taste but also controls glucose levels in diabetic patients very efficiently. foxtail millet chapatti may help to prevent from eating excess calories because of slower digestion rate and bulkiness of the fibres makes us feel fuller on, fewer calories .

3. Noodles – Vermicelli:

Millet vermicelli are staple food in many countries and changing food habits of children and teenaged groups have created a good market of noodles in world . Due to awareness of its nutritional properties demand for millet noodles is increasing s . Different combinations of Noodles are prepared, exclusively made of foxtail millet, foxtail millet and wheat in the ratio of 1:1 and foxtail millet blended with wheat and soy flour in the ratio of 5:4:1.Noodles are the pasta like products also known as convenience foods prepared through cold extrusion system which become hard and brittle after drying. The cooking of these noodles is very convenient and requires few minutes.

4.Bakery Products

Now a day’s peoples have no time to invest much on making breakfast it is the ready to eat food like cookie, bread/bun, cake etc., which had occurred instead of other sorts of stuff. Consumers are prefering healthy products to conventional foods, hence are these days showing preference to millet based bakery foods.
