History

Foxtail millet was first domesticated in China 8000 years ago and then became a cereal cultivated throughout Eurasia. It was cultivated in China by Peiligang culture as shown by earliest evidence but foxtail millet became the predominant grain only with the Yangshao culture . Green foxtail is the ancestor of cultivated foxtail millet according to evidences domestication began from around 16,000 YBP , was a recognized crop around 9000-10,000 YBP , and became popular in Northern China . Foxtail millet is cultivated in the developing countries in semiarid and arid regions . The earliest evidence for foxtail millet in East Siberia comes from the archaeological site and evidence in Korea come from a Jeulmun site in southern Korea . Foxtails are known for their spikelet clusters of bristled seeds , genus Setaria, also known as bristlegrass. The primary difference between the wild and cultivated forms of foxtail millet is their seed size . Foxtail millet also reached Taiwan ( 2800 BC )and the Tibetan Plateau (3000 BC) . The most primitive evidence of origin of foxtail millet in East Siberia found from the archaeological site at Krounovka in Primorsky Krai .
