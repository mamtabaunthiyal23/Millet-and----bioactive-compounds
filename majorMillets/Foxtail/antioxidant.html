Antioxidant

Antioxidants are man - made and natural substance that protects cells from damage caused by free radicals. P. Florence Suma and Asna Urooj studied antioxidant activity of extracts from foxtail millet (Setaria italica) , antioxidants were extracted using methanol, ethanol and water and Methanolic extracts of whole foxtail millet flour and bran rich fraction exhibited a significantly higher radical scavenging activity using DPPH model system . Highest antioxidant activity showed by bran rich fraction , so the presence of antioxidant components in the bran layer [1]. Meng Zhang et.al studied Comparative evaluation on phenolic profiles, antioxidant properties and α-glucosidase inhibitory effects of different milling fractions of foxtail millet were investigated . The bran of foxtail millet contained the highest free total phenolic content (TPC) and 18 phenolic compounds in the free phenolic extracts and 13 in the bound phenolic extracts from the different milling fractions of the foxtail millet were identified.For the free phenolic extracts, p-hydroxybenzaldehyde, vanillic acid and p-coumaric acid were the predominant phenolics in the husk. The antioxidant activities of the free and bound phenolic extracts in the husk and bran were significantly higher [2] .
