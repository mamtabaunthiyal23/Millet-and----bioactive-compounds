Production

In India, Karnataka is the major producer of finger millet accounting to 58% of its global production . In India finger millet stands on sixth position on production after wheat, rice, maize, sorghum and bajra.

In world, finger millet stands on fourth place among millets after pearl millet and foxtail millet . It is widely cultivated in Africa and South Asia under extreme weather conditions and it is estimated that some 10% of the world's 30 million tons of millet produced is finger millet .

Value Added Products from Finger Millet:

Ragi is a great substitute for other starchy grains and it can be used in different ways. Ragi is most nutritious food and it is rich in carbohydrate , protein , calcium and other minerals [1] . Following examples are given below :-

Chapatti (Roti):

In the ratio of 7:3 wheat and finger millet are mixed for making chapatti (roti). Gluten content is reduced in this mixture while making of flattering chapatti is not affected. The color of ragi chapatti turns to slightly dark. Fortification of finger millet in chapattis is used for improving the taste but also controls glucose levels in diabetic patients very efficiently. Ragi chapatti may help to prevent from eating excess calories because of slower digestion rate and bulkiness of the fibres makes us feel fuller on, fewer calories . It is also helpful to the individuals having the problem of constipation.

Papad:

Papad from finger millet, 15-20% (w/w) flour is used along with black or green gram, rice and spices is also prepared in which it is used as base material. this flour is first cooked in water till it is gelatinized and dough is prepared for papad . From this dough thin sheet are prepared by rolling it and cutting into different shapes and sizes . . However it gives slightlys dark color to the papad as the pericarp is not separated out from the starch, which again upon frying or roasting turns to lighter with good consumer acceptability.

Puffing or Popping:

: It is popular traditional ready to eat food with delicious flavor. This process improves the nutritional value by inactivating some of the anti- nutritional factors and thereby enhancing the protein and carbohydrate digestibility. This process enhance the appearance, color, taste and aroma of the processed raw material . For puffing, the whole ragi grain is conditioned by mixing additional water and tempered for about 4-6 hours under shed. During this process, the sugar react with amino acids of the millet causing Millard reaction and as a result, a pleasant and highly desired aroma is developed. During this process, the vapour pressure of the grain increases and the moisture present in the grain turns into steam and gelatinization of the starch takes places and explodes. During this process as grains are dehydrated to extremely low level of moisture content and increase its shelf-life .

Malting – Weaning Food:

For infant feeding purpose traditionally the millet malt is utilized and also to prepare beverages either with milk of lukewarm water with the addition of sugar . Finger millet having good malting characteristics is used to prepare weaning foods and malting of ragi improves its digestibility, sensory and nutritional quality as well as has pronounced effect in lowering the antinutrients. Finger millet is resistant to fungal infection elaboration of alpha and beta amylase during germination and during desirable aroma is developed . For milk based beverage the malted weaning food is mixed with powdered sugar, milk powder or whole milk along with flavoring agents. Malting is a good source of nutrition and suitable for all the age groups.

Noodles – Vermicelli:

: Millet vermicelli are staple food in many countries and changing food habits of children and teenaged groups have created a good market of noodles in world . Due to awareness of its nutritional properties demand for millet noodles is increasing s . Different combinations of Noodles are prepared, exclusively made of finger millet, finger millet and wheat in the ratio of 1:1 and finger millet blended with wheat and soy flour in the ratio of 5:4:1.Noodles are the pasta like products also known as convenience foods prepared through cold extrusion system which become hard and brittle after drying. The cooking of these noodles is very convenient and requires few minutes.

Extruded Products:

This is a novel way of transforming ingredients into value added products. Extruded products prepared from different grains are gaining importance among the all age groups and their demand is growing, one such example is ‘Kurkure’, very popular among children. Finger millet flour like other preparations can be blended with other legume flour ingredients in appropriate proportion with further fortification of minerals and vitamins to design a balanced nutritional extruded food .

Fermented Foods:

Dosa and Idli are popular fermented foods in many parts of India and very common breakfast foods and evening meals in southern part of the country.By adding this millet in food it enhance taste and at the same time enriches the food value in terms of protein, calcium and fibre. Depending on taste and choice sprouted finger millets are used for fermented foods .

Ragi Soup:

: Ragi soup is prepared by mixing the ragi flour into water and stirring is used so that lump formation should not occur and the mix should leave a smooth and thick body. This mix is heated For 15-17 minutes under medium heat . . To avoid lump formation Continuous stirring is needed . Mix is removed from the heat after cooking then and mixed with curd and salt to taste. After leaving it for another 5 minutes it is ready to serve as warm.
