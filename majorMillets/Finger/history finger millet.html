History

Finger millet originated in East Africa and it is cultivated in Africa and india . It is commonly known as ragi . Finger millet was domesticated 5000 years ago in South Africa and after 3000 years ago it domesticated in india [1] . Finger millet can grow in warm temperate areas of Africa to Japan and Australia and can also grow in colder areas of Northern Ireland, during summer. Finger millet grows very fast within 3 to 6 months it gets maturity and occasionally in only 45 days [2]. Usually it is found at altitudes between 1000 and 2000 in eastern and southern Africa, and up to 2500-3000 m in the Himalayas [2]. It grows best at an average temperature of 23Â°C but can also grow in extreme weather conditions . An annual rainfall is required for growing season is ranging from 500 to 1000 mm . Finger millet can grow on drier conditions and it is intolerant of flooded conditions . Ragi does not grow well in of heavy rain areas . Finger millet prefers fertile soil , well-drained sandy to sandy loam soils with a pH ranging from 5 to 7.

E. coracana subsp. africana is nearest wild relative of finger millet which is native of Africa [3]. It is cultivated in eastern and southern Africa as well as in the Indian sub-continent. It is grown on a small scale in South - East Asia thereby not attaining commercial importance. Subspecies of Eleusine is widely grown along the highlands of East Africa. So , it was concluded that finger millet originated in the East African highlands and domesticated in India. African and Asian finger millet has large diversity in their germplasm . Finger millet in the Indian sub-continent accompanied by natural mutations and human selection has resulted large diversity in landraces and local cultivars.
