Cultivation

Ragi grown from May to September and than crop is dried in sun for 2 to 3 days after cutting . Under irrigation or transplantation ragi is monocropped in India. It is mostly intercropped with cereals, castor bean, niger, groundnut and pulses. Fieldbean , pigeon pea , cowpea , and niger are most common crops which are grown with ragi . Finger millet is auxiliary crop with groundnuts.

Climatic requirements for ragi cultivation

For best growth, the finger millet cultivation requires daytime temperatures of 30 °C to 34 °C and night temperatures of 22°C to 25 °C with medium annual rainfall .

Soil requirements

For Finger millet cultivation porous/light red loam/sandy loam soils are ideal and it grows best on pH 4.5 to 8.0 .

Land preparation

The primary field should be ploughed 2 or 3 times with 80 cm rainfall for maintaining the moisture content of soil.

Sowing Methods and Propagation

Seeds are mainly used for propagation in finger millet agriculture. In rainfed cultivation the following sowing methods are employed:

  • Broadcasting: Seeds are immediately sown in the field by this method .
  • Line Sowing: Seeds are planted in lines in this method . Between lines 22 cm to 30 com line spacing should be done and 8 cm to 10 cm inside lines when using this method. Ragi seeds should be put in the soil around 3 cm deep.
  • Drilling in rows: This method is used to sow seeds. Seed cum fertiliser drill planting is highly advantageous in line sowing .
  • Seedling Transplantation: The procedure of planting seedlings in nursery beds and transplanting them to the main field is known as transplanting. In transplantation process , the beds must be watered. Sprout should be transferred into the field when they are four weeks old. Sprouts should be transplanted at 25 × 10 cm in the early Rabi and Kharif seasons, and at 30 cm x 10 cm in the late Kharif season. In 3 cm depth of soil planting should take place .

Seed Rate

Seed rate for ragi cultivation is around 10 to 15 kg per hectare.

Intercropping

Intercropping always yields good results and there are some best combinations such as ; Finger Millet + maize, Finger Millet + field bean , Finger Millet + Pigeon pea and Finger Millet + mustard.

Irrigation

  • Red soil:
    • 1st irrigation :- It should be done immediately after Sowing.
    • 2nd irrigation :- It should be done on the 3rd day after sowing.
    • 3rd irrigation :- It should be done on the 7th day after sowing .
    • 4th irrigation :- It should be done on the 12th day after sowing .
    • 5th irrigation :- It should be done on the 17th day.
  • Heavy soil :
    • 1st irrigation :- It should be done immediately after sowing.
    • 2nd irrigation :- It should be done on the 4th day after sowing .
    • 3rd irrigation :- sowing after 9th day after sowing .
    • 4th irrigation :- should be done on the 16th day after sowing.

    Weed Control

    Hand weed should be applied twice on the 9th and 19th days after transplanting the seedlings . Inter cultivation and weeding should be done after 4 weeks of seeding . Thinning should be done two weeks after seeding because finger millet is a densely seeded crop.

    Pests and Diseases

    Aphids, White stem borer, Root aphid, Finger Millet leaf hopper , Pink stem borer, Leaf folder, Earhead bug, Earhead bug, Cutworm, Yellow aphid, and Grasshopper are the most common pests encountered in Finger Millet cultivation. Blast and Mosaic are principal diseases in the Finger Millet crop .

    Harvesting

    Finger millet harvest will be ready and mature in 4 to 5 months and in 2 to 3 months, finger millet begins to flower. Ragi should be cut above ground level and dried for 2 to 3 days in the sun. Separating the seeds from the plants can be done with bullocks or sticks.

    Yield

    Yield is always affected by the cultivar used and crop management practises . If Ragi is grown as a rainfed crop, an average yield of 12 to 15 quintals/ha and irrigated crops can yield 40 to 45 quintals per hectare.
