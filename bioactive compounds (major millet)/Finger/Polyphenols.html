Polyphenols present in Finger millet

S.No. COMPOUND NAME IUPAC NAME MOLECULAR FORMULA STRUCTURE SYNONYMS SOURCES HEALTH BENEFITS REFERENCE
1 Gallic acid 3,4,5-trihydroxybenzoic acid C7H6O5 Benzoic acid, 3,4,5-trihydroxy-Gallic acid, Pyrogallol-5-carboxylic acid, Kyselina gallova, 3,4,5-Trihydroxybenzoate, 05 CHEBI:30778, AI3-16412, Kyselina 3,4,5-trihydroxybenzoova etc. Blueberry, blackberry, strawberry, plums, grapes, mango, cashew nut, hazelnut, walnut, tea, wine. Therapeutic activities in gastrointestinal, neuropsychological, metabolic, and cardiovascular disorders. [1,2]
2 Vanillic acid 4-hydroxy-3-methoxybenzoic acid C8H8O4 Acide vanllique Benzoic aci, 4-hydroxy-3-methoxy- p-Vanillic acid, 4-hydroxy- Protocatechuic acid, 3-methyl ester 4-hydroxy-3-methoxy-Benzoic acid etc. It is found in several fruits, olives, and cereal grains (e.g., whole wheat), as well as in wine, beer, and cider. Vanillic acid exerts diverse bioactivity against cancer, diabetes, obesity, neurodegenerative, cardiovascular, and hepatic diseases by inhibition of the associated molecular pathways. [3,4]
3 Protocatechuic acid 3,4-dihydroxybenzoic acid C7H6O4 Benzoic acid, 3,4-dihydroxy- 4,5-Dihydroxybenzoic acid 3,4-Dihydroxybenzoicacid,4-Carboxy-1,2-dihydroxybenzene Protocatehuic acid. Protocatechuic acid (PCA) is a phenolic compound commonly found in a wide range of spices, including garlic, onion, star anise, olive and chicory; vegetables, such as cauliflower and bitter melon; and as a primary metabolite of anthocyanins in human intestines. Protocatechuic acid exerts multiple biological properties, such as anticancer, anti-atherosclerosis, antidiabetes, and neuroprotection. [5]
4 Catechin (2R,3S)-2-(3,4-dihydroxyphenyl)-3,4-dihydro-2H-chromene-3,5,7-triol C15H14O6 Catechuic acid, Catechinic acid, Cyanidanol, D-Catechin,Cianidol, (+)-Catechol, (+)-Cyanidanol, Biocatechin. High concentrations of catechin can be found in red wine, broad beans, black grapes, apricots and strawberries; epicatechin concentrations are high in apples, blackberries, broad beans, cherries, black grapes, pears, raspberries, and chocolate. It has been proved that catechins could inhibit carcinogenesis, tumor growth, cancer cell invasion, and tumor angiogenesis, by suppressing the induction of proangiogenic factors, such as vascular endothelial growth factor (VEGF). [6]
5 Quercetin 2-(3,4-dihydroxyphenyl)-3,5,7-trihydroxychromen-4-one C15H10O7 Meletin, Sophoretin, Quercetine, Xanthaurine, Quercetol, Quertine. Fruits and vegetables are the primary dietary sources of quercetin, particularly citrus fruits, apples, onions, parsley, sage, tea, and red wine. Olive oil, grapes, dark cherries, and dark berries such as blueberries, blackberries, and bilberries are also high in quercetin and other flavonoids. A diet rich in quercetin has various health-promoting benefits. It acts as an agent to lower coagulation, hyperglycemia, inflammation, and hypertension. Various clinical studies show that supplementation of quercetin is used to prevent and treat various chronic diseases such as cardiovascular disorders. [7]
6 Homovanillic acid 2-(4-hydroxy-3-methoxyphenyl)acetic acid C9H10O4 4-Hydroxy-3-methoxyphenylacetic acid, 2-(4-hydroxy-3-methoxyphenyl)acetic acid, Vanillacetic acid, Vanilacetic acid, Benzeneacetic acid, 4-hydroxy-3-methoxy- 3-Methoxy-4-hydroxyphenylacetic acid, Homovanillate . Homovanillic acid is a natural product found in Aloe africana, Ginkgo biloba, and other organisms with data available. Homovanillic acid is a dopamine metabolite found to be associated with aromatic L-amino acid decarboxylase deficiency, celiac disease, growth hormone deficiency, and sepiapterin reductase deficiency. [8]
7 Naringin (2S)-7-[(2S,3R,4S,5S,6R)-4,5-dihydroxy-6-(hydroxymethyl)-3-[(2S,3R,4R,5R,6S)-3,4,5-trihydroxy-6-methyloxan-2-yl]oxyoxan-2-yl]oxy-5-hydroxy-2-(4-hydroxyphenyl)-2,3-dihydrochromen-4-one C27H32O14 Naringoside, aurantiin, Naringenin 7-O-neohesperidoside, 4'5-diOH-Flavone-7-rhgluc Naringenin is a flavonoid belonging to flavanones subclass. It is widely distributed in several Citrus fruits, bergamot, tomatoes and other fruits, being also found in its glycosides form (mainly naringin). Naringenin is endowed with broad biological effects on human health , which includes a decrease in lipid peroxidation biomarkers and protein carbonylation, promotes carbohydrate metabolism, increases antioxidant defenses, scavenges reactive oxygen species, modulates immune system activity, and also exerts anti-atherogenic and anti-inflammatory effects. [9]
8 Taxifolin (2R,3R)-2-(3,4-dihydroxyphenyl)-3,5,7-trihydroxy-2,3-dihydrochromen-4-one C15H12O7 Taxifoliol, (2R,3R)-Dihydroquercetin 24198-97-8, (+)-Dihydroquercetin . It is found in onion, milk thistle, French maritime pine bark and Douglas fir bark. It is also used in various commercial preparations like Legalon™, Pycnogenol®, and Venoruton®. Taxifolin has shown to have anti-proliferative effects on many types of cancer cells by inhibiting cancer cell lipogenesis. By inhibiting the fatty acid synthase in cancer cells, taxifolin is able to prevent the growth and spread of cancer cells. [10]
9 Ferulic acid (E)-3-(4-hydroxy-3-methoxyphenyl)prop-2-enoic acid C10H10O4 4-Hydroxy-3-methoxycinnamic acid, trans-4-Hydroxy-3-methoxycinnamic acid, 3-(4-Hydroxy-3-methoxyphenyl)acrylic acid, (E)-Ferulic acid, Coniferic acid . It is commonly found in commelinid plants (rice, wheat, oats, and pineapple), grasses, grains, vegetables, flowers, fruits, leaves, beans, seeds of coffee, artichoke, peanut and nuts. It exhibits a wide range of therapeutic effects against various diseases like cancer, diabetes, cardiovascular and neurodegenerative. [11]
10 p-Coumaric acid (E)-3-(4-hydroxyphenyl)prop-2-enoic acid C9H8O3 p-Hydroxycinnamic acid, trans-4-Hydroxycinnamic acid, 4-Coumaric acid, 3-(4-hydroxyphenyl)acrylic acid, Para-Coumaric acid, Hydroxycinnamic acid. p-Coumaric acid can be found in a wide variety of edible plants and fungi such as peanuts, navy beans, tomatoes, carrots, basil and garlic. It is found in wine and vinegar. It is also found in barley grain. p-Coumaric acid can attenuate skin hyperpigmentation through multiple mechanisms. UV and other pathophysiological conditions stimulate the production of reactive oxygen species (ROS) and multiple signaling pathways leading to enhanced gene expression of tyrosinase and increased melanin synthesis. [12]
