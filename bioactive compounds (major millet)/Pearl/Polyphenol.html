1 Gallic acid 3,4,5-trihydroxybenzoic acid C7H6O5 Benzoic acid, 3,4,5-trihydroxy-Gallic acid, Pyrogallol-5-carboxylic acid, Kyselina gallova, 3,4,5-Trihydroxybenzoate, CHEBI:30778, AI3-16412, Kyselina 3,4,5-trihydroxybenzoova etc. Blueberry, blackberry, strawberry, plums, grapes, mango, cashew nut, hazelnut, walnut, tea, wine. Therapeutic activities in gastrointestinal, neuropsychological, metabolic, and cardiovascular disorders. [1,2]
2 Quercetin 2-(3,4-dihydroxyphenyl)-3,5,7-trihydroxychromen-4-one C15H10O7 Meletin, Sophoretin, Quercetine, Xanthaurine, Quercetol, Quertine. Fruits and vegetables are the primary dietary sources of quercetin, particularly citrus fruits, apples, onions, parsley, sage, tea, and red wine. Olive oil, grapes, dark cherries, and dark berries such as blueberries, blackberries, and bilberries are also high in quercetin and other flavonoids. A diet rich in quercetin has various health-promoting benefits. It acts as an agent to lower coagulation, hyperglycemia, inflammation, and hypertension. Various clinical studies show that supplementation of quercetin is used to prevent and treat various chronic diseases such as cardiovascular disorders. [3]
3 p-Coumaric acid (E)-3-(4-hydroxyphenyl)prop-2-enoic acid C9H8O3 p-Hydroxycinnamic acid, trans-4-Hydroxycinnamic acid, 4-Coumaric acid, 3-(4-hydroxyphenyl)acrylic acid, Para-Coumaric acid, Hydroxycinnamic acid. p-Coumaric acid can be found in a wide variety of edible plants and fungi such as peanuts, navy beans, tomatoes, carrots, basil and garlic. It is found in wine and vinegar. It is also found in barley grain. p-Coumaric acid can attenuate skin hyperpigmentation through multiple mechanisms. UV and other pathophysiological conditions stimulate the production of reactive oxygen species (ROS) and multiple signaling pathways leading to enhanced gene expression of tyrosinase and increased melanin synthesis. [4]
4 Ascorbic acid (2R)-2-[(1S)-1,2-dihydroxyethyl]-3,4-dihydroxy-2H-furan-5-one C6H8O6 vitamin C, L-ascorbate, L(+)-Ascorbic acid, ascorbate, Ascoltin, Ascorbicap, Cevitamic acid, Cenolate, Hybrin . Citrus fruits, tomatoes and tomato juice, and potatoes are major contributors of vitamin C to the American diet. Other good food sources include red and green peppers, kiwifruit, broccoli, strawberries, Brussels sprouts, and cantaloupe. Vitamin C, also known as ascorbic acid, has several important functions. These include: helping to protect cells and keeping them healthy maintaining healthy skin, blood vessels, bones and cartilage helping with wound healing. [5]
5 Cinnamic acid (E)-3-phenylprop-2-enoic acid C9H8O2 3-Phenylpropenoic acid, (2E)-3-phenylprop-2-enoic acid, trans-beta-Carboxystyrene, 3-phenylprop-2-enoic acid, 2-Propenoic acid, 3-phenyl-, (2E)- Cinnamic acid, (E)- Cinnamylic acid. Cinnamic acid can be generally obtained from cinnamon , citrus fruits, grape , tea , cocoa , spinach , celery , and brassicas vegetables Cinnamic acid is beneficial as antioxidant , anti-inflammatory , and anti-cancer activities. [6]
6 Benzoic acid benzoic acid C7H6O2 Benzenecarboxylic acid, Carboxybenzene, Benzeneformic acid, phenylformic acid, Benzenemethanoic acid, Phenylcarboxylic acid, Retardex, Benzoesaeure GK, Benzoesaeure GV . Benzoic Acid is found naturally in many foods such as apples, apricots, snap beans, cranberries and cinnamon leaf/bark. Benzoic acid is a commonly used antimicrobial preservative in food and beverages, especially in carbonated beverages, as it presents its strongest antibacterial activity at pH 2.5–4.0. BA has inhibitory effects on the proliferation of bacteria and yeasts, a major cause of food spoilage. [7]
7 Catechol benzene-1,2-diol C6H6O2 1,2-dihydroxybenzene, 1,2-benzenediol, benzene-1,2-diol, pyrocatechin, 2-hydroxyphenol, o-Benzenediol, o-Dihydroxybenzene, o-Dioxybenzene, o-Hydroquinone. Catechol has since been shown to occur in free form naturally in kino and in beechwood tar. Catechol is used in a variety of applications. It is used as a reagent for photography, dyeing fur, rubber and plastic production and in the pharmaceutical industry . [8]
