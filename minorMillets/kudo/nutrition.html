Nutrition

Kodo millet is considered a "nutria-cereal" due to its richness in vitamins, minerals, and phytochemicals, including sulfur-containing compounds. It is also a great source of essential amino acids such as lysine, threonine, valine, and sulfur-containing amino acids. The ratio of leucine to isoleucine in kodo millet is around 2.0, but it is deficient in tryptophan. In addition, kodo millet is abundant in vitamin B3, vitamin B6, and folic acid, as well as essential minerals like calcium, potassium, magnesium, and zinc. Kodo millet grains contain 8.3% protein, with glutelin being the major protein component.
