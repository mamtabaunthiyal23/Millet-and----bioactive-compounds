Production

Fonio, a small grain crop that is mainly grown and consumed in West African countries, has shown resilient production growth over the years. In 2021, its global production was estimated to be worth $X in export price, which marks a decline compared to the previous year's peak production level of $X. Although the yield per hectare in India, one of the countries where fonio is cultivated, remained relatively unchanged at less than X kg per ha, future yields may still be subject to adverse weather conditions despite the increased use of modern agricultural techniques. Guinea is the largest producer of fonio in the world, accounting for over 75% of global production in 2019. The global fonio market was estimated to be 673,000 tonnes in 2016. Fonio's popularity has been on the rise, and in December 2018, the European Commission authorized its commercialization as a novel food in the European Union. Obà Food, an Italian company, submitted fonio for approval, which has paved the way for the manufacture and marketing of new food products.

Value added products

  • Vermicelli:-

Fonio Obà pasta is a wholesome and healthy choice as it is made solely from water and flour, preserving the original delicate aroma of the grain that has been cultivated for over 5,000 years. Unlike other grains, Fonio has not been genetically modified or subjected to laboratory processes, making it a pure and ancient grain. It is primarily cultivated by small rural communities in the arid sub-Saharan region, and therefore not a result of intensive agricultural practices that can have a detrimental environmental impact. Fonio Obà pasta stands out for its versatility, delicate taste, and high nutritional value. It is an excellent source of proteins, fibers, B vitamins, iron, and zinc, with a low glycemic index and nickel content. Fonio's rich nutritional profile makes it a perfect choice for a healthy diet, and its natural cultivation process further enhances its health benefits.

  • Brooklyn Brewery brews

    • Brooklyn Brewery has teamed up with Yolélé to launch a unique brew that features a super cool ingredient. The Brooklyn Brewery x Yolélé Fonio White Beer is brewed with fonio, an ancient West African grain that is highly nutritious and has been introduced to the American market by Yolélé. Both Yolélé and Brooklyn Brewery are passionate about promoting sustainable and responsible practices in the food and beverage industry. The beer boasts a beautiful golden hue, thanks to the tiny blond fonio grains that are packed with essential nutrients and health benefits. The collaboration between these two companies highlights their shared commitment to sourcing ingredients strategically and promoting sustainability.
