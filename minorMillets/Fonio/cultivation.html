Cultivation

Climate requirement

Fonio, a type of grain, is primarily grown in West Africa where the climate is tropical with a distinct dry season. The typical temperatures in the region range from 25 to 30 °C, and annual rainfall averages between 600 to 1200 mm. However, Fonio is also cultivated at higher altitudes, where conditions differ. In these areas, located over 1000 m above sea level, annual rainfall is typically higher, ranging from 1200 to 1500 mm, and temperatures are cooler, averaging between 15 to 25 °C. Despite these variations, Fonio continues to thrive in both environments.

Soil requirement

The root system of Fonio is extensive, and it can grow to depths of over one meter in the soil. This root system is a key factor in the plant's ability to perform well during the dry season and to adapt to poor and low fertility soils. While Fonio is typically grown on sandy soils, it can also thrive on rocky soils. It is also well-suited to acidic clay soils with high aluminium content. However, on heavy soils, most varieties of Fonio tend to perform poorly.

Field management

Throughout Africa, most of the work is done by hand.

Before sowing

Pests

Insect pests include:

  • Shoot flies, Atherigona spp.
  • Stem borer, Chilo partellus

Harvest and post-harvest processing

Fonio, also known as Digitaria exilis, is an important cereal crop in West Africa that has been traditionally harvested and processed by hand. Men cut the fonio with sickles while women and children gather it into sheaves. Threshing is also done manually, and hulls are removed using mortars. However, industrial machinery has been developed to replace the traditional, labor-intensive process. While there are potential economic benefits to using these machines, they come at a high cost and require multiple villages to operate them together. Digitaria exilis has many qualities that make it a good crop candidate, including its high levels of carbohydrate and protein, with a rich source of essential amino acids such as methionine. It is also adapted to marginal land and requires little input to grow, making it a potential good food source. However, the crop struggles with competing with weeds and requires fallow time after harvest for soil nutrient recovery.
