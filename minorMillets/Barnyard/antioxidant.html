Antioxidants

Antioxidants play a crucial role in maintaining physiological processes by reducing DNA damage and lipid peroxidation. They also help regulate the immune system and prevent malignant cell transformation. Several antioxidant components, including polyphenols, phenolic acid, and flavonoids, are capable of scavenging free radicals such as peroxides and hydroperoxides, thereby preventing oxidative mechanisms that can cause degenerative diseases. Barnyard millet is a rich source of phytochemical components such as dietary fibers, soluble and insoluble fractions. In this study, Murugan et.al investigated the phytochemical content, total phenolic content, and antioxidant capacities of various forms of barnyard millet [1] .

Free radicals are highly reactive oxygen species that can be generated in our body due to various physiological and environmental factors. Maintaining a balance between free radicals and antioxidants is crucial for optimal physiological function. Barnyard millet is a highly nutritious food that has shown promise in managing diabetes mellitus, hyperlipidemia, and other health conditions. This study aimed to analyze the presence of phytochemicals, total phenolic content, and antioxidant activity in different forms of barnyard millet, including raw, sprouted, and soaked millet. Phytochemical analysis was performed for alkaloids, phenols, flavonoids, tannins, terpenoids, saponins, proteins, and carbohydrates. The results showed that the methanol extract of soaked millet had the highest total phenolic content of 54.5±0.00mg of GAE/g and the highest DPPH radical scavenging activity of 87.90%. Interestingly, the DPPH scavenging activity was high in raw millet and low in sprouted and soaked millet. These findings suggest that barnyard millet could be a valuable source of antioxidants. Further studies are warranted to determine the potential health benefits of consuming barnyard millet in different forms and to elucidate the underlying mechanisms of its antioxidant activity.
