Cultivation

Season

  • Odisha - Middle of June
  • Tamil Nadu - June and September-October
  • Karnataka, Madhya Pradesh and South Bihar - last week of June to first week of July is desirable to escape from shoot fly and gall midge.

Spacing and seed rate

Spacing : Spacing should be 22.5 cm between rows and 10 cm between plants within a row.

Seed rate:

  • For line sowing 8 kg per ha
  • 1 For broadcasting 2 kg per ha

Manuring and fertilization

  • 5 tones of farm yard manure per hectare 2-3 weeks before sowing.
  • The fertilizer required for different states is as follows

Bio-fertilizer:Seed inoculation with Agrobacterium radiobacter and Aspergillus awamori improves seed yield.

Weed control

  • Two inter cultivations and one hand weeding in line sowing.
  • Two hand weeding in broadcasted crop

Cropping systems

Inter cropping
  • Odisha : Little millet + black gram
  • Madhya Pradesh: Little millet + Sesamum
  • Southern Bihar: Little millet + pigeon pea

Diseases

While this crop generally does not suffer from severe diseases, grain smut can occasionally be a concern. However, the problem of grain smut can be effectively addressed through pre-sowing treatment of seeds. Specifically, treating seeds with either Carbendazim or Carboxin at a rate of 2 grams per kilogram of seeds has been shown to be effective in preventing grain smut.

Pests

Shootfly

Most serious pest causing significant yield losses.

Control :

Early sowing with the onset of monsoon is an effective and cheapest method of control.
