Types of Millets(On the basis of size of grain )

On the basis of size of grains millets are classifeied into 2 groups .

  1. Major millet
  2. Minor millet
  1. Major millet
  • Finger Millet ( Eleusine Coracana)
  • Pearl millet (Pennisetum glaucum )
  • Foxtail millet (Setaria italica )
  • Proso millet (Panicum milliaceum )
  • 2. Minor millet
    • Kodo millet (Paspalum scrobiculatum)
    • Little millet (Panicum sumatrense )
    • Barnyard millet ( Echinochloa frumentacea)
    • Fonio millet (Digitaria exilis)
    • Teff millet (Eragrostis tef )
    1. Major millet
        2. (i) Finger Millet ( Eleusine Coracana)

      Finger Millet is popularly known as Ragi and Nachani . Karnataka is the state in india which produce large amount of Ragi . It is commonly consumed by fitness enthusiasts because it helps in maintaining a healthy body weight. It is a gluten-free , rich in proteins , vitamins , minerals and amino acids. For growing children, ragi is intended to facilitate brain growth. .

        (ii) Pearl millet (Pennisetum glaucum )

      Pearl millet is commonly known as Bajra . Pearl millet is rich in nutrients such as protrin , fibre iron and minerals . Pearl millet is gluten free and it has highest amount of folic acid which is beneficial for pregnant womens diet . Pearl millets are rich source of antioxidants . Consumption of Pearl millet provide us protection against cancer, arthritis, cardiovascular disease and diabetes .

        (iii) Foxtail millet (Setaria italica )

      Foxtail Millet is commonly known as Kakum/Kangni in India. It is beneficial for pregnant womens because of its high fibre amount which can help in good digestion and avoid constipation which is common during pregnancy . High amount of carbohydrates in foxtail millet helps in balancing blood sugar levels in the body. Foxtail Millet , improve glycemic control and reduce insulin, cholesterol and fasting glucose in Type-2 diabetes patients.

        (iv) Proso millet (Panicum milliaceum )

      Proso millet is commonly known as chena or barri. Proso millet help to lower bad cholesterol which is beneficial for healthy heart. It prevents Pellagra which is skin disorder in this disorder skin becomes dry and rough . Proso millet is rich in nutrients such as proteins , minerals , fibre and carbohydrates . Daily intake of proso millet can reduce the risk of Type 2 diabetes . Proso millets is beneficial in anti agging because of antioxidants.

    2. Minor millet
      (i) Kodo millet (Paspalum scrobiculatum)

    Kodo Millet is commonly known as Kodon / Kodra. Kodo millet is used as home remedy for healing external wound . Daily intake of kodo millet is useful for postmenopausal women suffering from signs of cardiovascular diseases . kodo millet is rich in fibre content , it helps to reudce digestive problems such as bloating , constipation . Kodo millet is rich in nutrients such as protein , carbohydrate , minerals and vitamins .

      (ii) Little millet (Panicum sumatrense )

    Little millet is commonly known as Kutki, Shavan . Little millet is rich in nuterients such as carbohydrates , protein , minerals and vitamins . Kutki is also rich in niacin which helps to reduce bad cholesterol . It prevents diseases like diabetes, cardiovascular diseases, cancer, inflammation, Gastrointestinal problems .

      (iii) Barnyard millet ( Echinochloa frumentacea)

    Barnyard millet is commonly known as Jhangora . It is a good source of protein, carbohydrate, fiber, and, minerals. It is rich in calcium and phosphorus, which can strengthen bone density. Barnyard millet can be provided to 6 to 8 months baby as kheer. barnyard millet can be used as food and fodder . It has low Glycemic index and it is gluten free food .

      (iv) Fonio millet (Digitaria exilis)

    Fonio millet is also known as hungry millet . Fonio millet is divided into 2 types : White and Black Fonio millet . Fonio millet is rich in nuterients such as carbohydrates , dietary fibre , protein and vitamins. It helps to reduced risk of type 2 diabetes , heart diseases , pancreatic and stomach cancer . It is gluten free and has low glycemic index . Fonio millet helps to stay healthy hair and skin because of presence of iron and zinc and vitamins .

      (v) Teff millet (Eragrostis tef )

    Teﬀ is also known as williams lovegrass or annual bunch grass . Teff crop can be grown in rabi and kharif both seasons . Teff millet is rich in nutrients such as dietary fiber , protein, carbohydrate , vitamin and minerals . Teff millet is gluten-free . Teff millet is good for digestion because of presence of dietary fibre in it and it helps to prevent gastric problems.
