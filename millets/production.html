Production

In 2020, global production of millet was 30.5 million tonnes, led by India with 41% of the world total .

United Nations General Assembly on the Government of India's proposal (with the support of 72 countries) to the Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO) declared 2023 as the International Year of the Millets. The Department of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare signed an MoU with National Agricultural Cooperative Marketing Federation (NAFED) to promote and strengthen millet cultivation, production, packaging, and the overall supply chain at FAO’s recently organized ‘One Country, One Product’ Conference, India represented the case of millets. In the budget initiatives proposed for 2022, much focus has been given to the post-harvest value chain, processing, and packaging of millets – the most critical areas needing attention. ‘Rastriya Poshan Maah’ of the Ministry of Women and Child Development, with a focused approach to women empowerment based on millet-based foods, also contribute to food and nutrition security initiatives. The government realises millets’ enormous potential for maintaining agricultural diversity and environmental protection. For instance, growing nutrient-dense millets is less cost intensive.

Alcoholic beverages

Tongba, a millet-based alcoholic drink which is found in mountainous region of Nepal and Sikkim and Darjeeling . It is traditional drink and it is prepared in a round container .It helps in good digestion , boost immunity and helps a person to maintain his or her sugar level .
Tongba

As a food source

Millets are used as major food source from ancient time . In nowadays a lot of food products are made by millet such as ; chapati , papad , vermicelli , infants food ,puffs and fermented food(idli,dosa ). Millet porridge is a traditional food in Russian, German, and in other countries. In Russia, it is eaten with sugar and milk and in China it is eaten without sugar and milk .

Awaokoshi is a candied millet puffs , it is speciality of Japan .
